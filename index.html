Bankrupcy prediction with SAS

The bankruptcy prediction model leverages a dataset of 10,000 rows with 65 attributes, preprocessed to handle issues like missing values, duplicate records, and class imbalance. Applied logarithmic transformations to the financial measures for improved interpretability and normal distribution. Utilizing gradient boosting, the final model achieved a performance with an average squared error of 0.015 and a ROC index of 0.924, effectively distinguishing between bankrupt and non-bankrupt entities. The model's robustness was validated through public and private leaderboard evaluations, reflecting its strong predictive capabilities.
